🍽 food
☕ Breakfast — Chelsea Market
▼
75 9th Ave — Take the A/C/E to 14th St / 8th Ave. Chelsea Market is a converted Nabisco factory full of independent food stalls. Get coffee + a bagel or breakfast sandwich from the vendors inside. The building itself is architecturally amazing.

🌿 The High Line
▼
Enter at Gansevoort St (south end) and walk the 1.5-mile elevated park northward. Former freight rail line converted into an incredible park with city skyline views, public art, and curated plantings. Free to enter. Best in the morning before weekend crowds arrive.
11:30 AM
👣 explore
🏘 Meatpacking District + West Village
▼
Walk down from the High Line into the Meatpacking District — cobblestone streets, boutique shops, street art everywhere. Then wander into the West Village for some of the prettiest brownstone-lined blocks in the entire city.
1:00 PM
🍽 food
🌮 Lunch — Los Tacos No. 1 (Chelsea)
▼
Inside Chelsea Market. Corn tortillas, birria, al pastor — these are widely considered the best tacos in Manhattan. Very quick, very cheap, very local. Get the adobada (al pastor) and a agua fresca.
2:30 PM
👣 explore
🎨 SoHo Galleries + The Earth Room
▼
Walk West Broadway between Houston and Spring St — about a dozen galleries. Then stop at The New York Earth Room at 141 Wooster St: a room on the second floor of a narrow building, completely filled with 280,000 lbs of dirt. It's free, bizarre, and strangely moving. Walter De Maria's permanent installation since 1977.

🌳 Central Park
▼
Enter at 72nd St (West). Hit Strawberry Fields (John Lennon memorial), Bethesda Fountain & Terrace (iconic fountain from movies), and The Lake. Walk northeast toward the Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis Reservoir. Consider renting a Citi Bike to cover more ground — dock right inside the park entrance.
12:30 PM
🍽 food
🌭 Lunch — Gray's Papaya (72nd & Broadway)
▼
2090 Broadway at 72nd St — a true NYC institution. Two hot dogs + tropical papaya drink for about $6. Open 24 hours. NYC's most iconic cheap lunch. Locals have been eating here since 1973. Don't overthink it — just get the Recession Special.
1:30 PM
📍 landmark
🦕 American Museum of Natural History
▼
Central Park West at 79th St — right on the park's west side. YES — this is the actual Night at the Museum museum! Tickets ~$28. Must-sees: the massive 94-ft blue whale in Milstein Hall of Ocean Life, the T. rex skeleton, the Hall of Meteorites (you can touch a 4-billion-year-old meteorite!), and the Rose Center for Earth and Space / Hayden Planetarium. Budget 2.5–3 hours minimum.
4:30 PM
📍 landmark
⛴ Staten Island Ferry — Free Statue of Liberty Views
▼
Take the 1 train back downtown to South Ferry station. The Staten Island Ferry terminal is right there. Completely FREE, runs every 30 minutes. The ferry passes right by the Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island — incredible close-up views of Lady Liberty from the water without paying $25+ for an official tour. Ride it over and back (~50 min round trip). Golden hour in March makes this magical. One of NYC's best free experiences.

🍽 food
🍕 Lunch — Lombardi's, America's First Pizzeria (1905) Must Do
▼
32 Spring St, Little Italy — 10-min walk from SoHo. Opened in 1905, this is the original American pizzeria. Coal-fired brick oven, whole pies only (~$25+). Thin slightly charred crust, fresh whole-milk mozzarella, San Marzano tomatoes. No slices — just sit down and order a pie. Get the classic margherita. Between this and Prince Street Pizza on night 1, you'll have done NYC pizza from both ends of the spectrum.
1:30 PM
📍 landmark
🏙 Rockefeller Center + Top of the Rock
▼
Take the C/E uptown to 50th St. Walk the Rockefeller Center plaza (free) — see the Today Show studio windows, 30 Rock, St. Patrick's Cathedral on 5th Ave. Then go up Top of the Rock observatory (~$40, book online). 70 floors up with a 360° open-air view of Manhattan — Central Park stretching north, Empire State Building, the Hudson and East Rivers. Unlike the Empire State Building, from here you can actually SEE the Empire State Building in your photos. One of the best views in the city.

💳 Forget MetroCard — It's Gone

As of January 2026, MetroCards are no longer sold. The subway now runs entirely on OMNY — just tap your contactless credit card, Apple Pay, or Google Pay at the turnstile. That's it. No card to buy, no app to download. Subway fare is $3/ride.

🔑 Weekly Fare Cap — Free Rides After 12

Use the same card or device every time and once you hit $35 in fares for the week, all remaining rides are free. Perfect for 5 days of heavy sightseeing. Just don't switch between your phone and physical card — the cap tracks per payment method.

⏰ Avoid Rush Hour

Weekday rush: 8–9:30 AM and 4:30–7 PM. Trains get very packed. You're on vacation — sleep in a bit and you'll have a much better experience.

📱 Check for Weekend Service Changes

The MTA does track maintenance on weekends. Always check the MTA app Saturday/Sunday morning — lines sometimes skip stations or run differently. Citymapper automatically routes around changes in real time.

🚕 When to Just Take an Uber

Late at night after shows, when you have shopping bags, or if the subway isn't running your route. From Midtown back to SoHo should be $15–25 depending on time. Avoid Uber during rain — surge pricing gets wild.
